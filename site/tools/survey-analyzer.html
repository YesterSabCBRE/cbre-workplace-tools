📖 How to interpret this report
📊
Agreement Scale (1–4)
Most questions use a 4-point agreement scale: 4 = Strongly Agree down to 1 = Strongly Disagree (no neutral option). Averages above 3.0 indicate positive sentiment; below 2.5 flags a concern area. Charts display response distributions with the mean score shown on the right side.
🏆
Employee NPS (eNPS)
Derived from a 0–10 likelihood-to-recommend question. Promoters score 9–10, Passives 7–8, Detractors 0–6. NPS = % Promoters − % Detractors. A score above +30 is considered strong; above +50 is excellent. High passives (>40%) warrant attention — they can shift either way.
🔺
Engagement Pyramid
Three nested levels of workplace experience: Physical Environment — foundational space quality and functionality; Connections — team relationships and learning; Belonging & Values — culture, WVP, and organizational identity. Each level shows average Likert scores as a % of the 4-point maximum.
🏅
WVP Composite Score
The Workplace Value Proposition (WVP) measures the degree to which employees feel the organization delivers on its cultural promises. Nine dimensions are averaged (Belonging, Inclusivity, Recognition, etc.) and converted to a 0–100 scale. Scores ≥ 70 indicate strong attachment; 50–69 moderate; < 50 signals meaningful disengagement. Gaps between top and bottom dimensions highlight where cultural investment is most needed.
